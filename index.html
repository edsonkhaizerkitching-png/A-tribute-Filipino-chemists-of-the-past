A Tribute: Filipino Chemists of the Past

Alfredo C. Santos

Alfredo C. Santos was a prominent Filipino chemist and a National Scientist of the Philippines, recognized for his significant contributions to phytochemistry, which is the study of chemicals derived from plants. His research focused on isolating and characterizing alkaloids from native Philippine plants, which are compounds known for their medicinal properties.

His work had a direct impact on the community by paving the way for the development of new drugs and medicines from local resources. He was a pioneer in applying modern chemistry to traditional medicine, highlighting the potential of the country's biodiversity for pharmaceutical use.

Anacleto del Rosario

Anacleto del Rosario is known as the "Father of Philippine Science and Laboratory." His significant contributions include the development of a process to produce pure alcohol from tuba, a local palm wine. He also researched and created a method for extracting castor oil.

His work had a direct impact on the community by improving local industries and providing access to purer, more reliable products. He was a pioneer who laid the groundwork for future scientific research in the Philippines.

Baldomero Olivera

Baldomero Olivera is a Filipino chemist and a National Scientist of the Philippines. He is an internationally renowned scientist best known for his discovery of conotoxins, which are found in the venom of cone snails. These toxins are now widely used in neuroscience and have led to the development of several pain-relieving drugs.

His research had a global impact, opening up an entirely new field of study in biochemistry and neuropharmacology. Olivera's work has been a testament to the fact that Filipino scientists can contribute to major discoveries on a worldwide scale.

Clara Y. Lim-Sylianco

Clara Y. Lim-Sylianco was a distinguished Filipino chemist known for her research in organic chemistry, biochemistry, and genetics. She authored several books and more than 130 scientific papers in her long and prolific career.

Her most significant contributions were in the fields of environmental and genetic toxicology. Her research focused on the study of mutagens, which are agents that cause genetic mutations. Her work was instrumental in proving that many common Philippine food preservatives and pollutants could cause mutations and damage human cells. This research was groundbreaking and had a major impact on public health and safety in the country.

Francisco O. Santos

Francisco O. Santos was a Filipino agricultural chemist and a National Scientist of the Philippines. His research focused on the nutritional value of native Filipino foods and the impact of diet on the health of Filipinos. He was a pioneer in the field of human nutrition in the Philippines.

His contributions had a direct community impact by providing scientific data that promoted better health and nutrition practices. He analyzed the chemical composition of many local foods, like rice and sweet potato, and his findings were essential for public health programs and agricultural development.

Jose O. Juliano

Jose O. Juliano was a Filipino chemist known for his significant contributions to nuclear science and radiochemistry. His research focused on the peaceful and beneficial applications of nuclear technology in various fields.

His work had a profound community impact, especially in agriculture, where he used nuclear techniques to improve crop yields and quality. He also applied his expertise to industrial processes and medical diagnostics, pioneering the use of nuclear technology to address local challenges and benefit Filipino society.

Julian Banzon

Julian Banzon was a Filipino chemist and a National Scientist of the Philippines, renowned for his research on the production of fuels and chemicals from local crops. His work focused on finding innovative ways to use native plants as an alternative to imported resources.

His most notable contribution was his pioneering work on producing ethyl esters from sugarcane, which could be used as a biofuel. He also conducted extensive research on the extraction of coconut oil and its potential for use in a wide range of products, including gasoline. His work directly contributed to the development of sustainable, locally-sourced fuel and other products, demonstrating the power of chemistry to benefit the community.

Lourdes J. Cruz

Lourdes J. Cruz is a renowned Filipino biochemist, famous for her work on conotoxins, which are powerful neurotoxins found in the venom of cone snails. Her research has been instrumental in understanding the biochemistry of these toxins, which has led to significant advancements in neuroscience and medicine.

Her work has had a profound impact on the scientific community. The discovery of these unique peptides has opened new avenues for developing drugs for pain management and treating neurological disorders. Her contributions have not only advanced the field of biochemistry in the Philippines but have also put the country on the map as a center for marine biology and scientific research.

Maria Orosa e Ylagan

María Orosa e Ylagan, was a Filipina food technologist, pharmaceutical chemist, humanitarian, and war heroine. She experimented with foods native to the Philippines, and during World War II, she developed Soyalac (a nutrient-rich drink from soybeans) and darak (cookies made from rice bran, packed with vitamin B-1, which prevents beriberi disease), which she also helped smuggle into Japanese-run internment camps that helped save the lives of thousands of Filipinos, Americans, and other nationals.

During World War II, Orosa joined Marking's Guerrillas to fight for Philippines freedom. She invented over 700 recipes during her lifetime, including Soyalac and Darak, which saved thousands of lives during the war. She also invented a process for canning goods for the guerrilla warriors fighting for the liberation of the Philippines. Without her food inventions, thousands of people would have perished in the war.

Pio Andrade

​Pio Andrade was a Filipino chemist known for his extensive research in food technology, radiation chemistry, and pesticide biodegradation. He earned his Bachelor of Science in Chemistry from the Mapúa Institute of Technology and dedicated his career to applied chemical research.

​His contributions had a direct impact on the community by addressing practical issues in agriculture and industry. His research on pesticide biodegradation and biomass energy demonstrated his commitment to finding solutions for environmental and energy challenges in the Philippines.
